About the Island

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano.

Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most of the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Amenities & Visitor Information

  • Power outlets: 120 volts (same as the United States)
  • Currency: Taniti uses the U.S. dollar. Many businesses also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.
  • Alcohol: Not allowed to be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 a.m. The drinking age is 18 (not strictly enforced).
  • Language: Many younger Tanititans speak fluent English. Very little English is spoken in rural areas.
  • Medical: There is one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees.
  • Safety: Violent crime is very rare, but as tourism increases there are more reports of pickpocketing and petty crime.
  • Holidays: Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays. Many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on holidays, so visitors should plan accordingly.
