About the Island

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano.

Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most of the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

[PHOTO OF ISLAND AMENITIES]
  • Power outlets: 120 volts (same as the United States)
  • Currency: U.S. dollar accepted. Many businesses also accept euros and yen. Major credit cards widely accepted.
  • Alcohol: Not served between midnight and 9:00 a.m. Drinking age is 18.
  • Language: Many younger Tanititans speak fluent English. Little English spoken in rural areas.
  • Medical: One hospital and several clinics with multilingual staff.
  • Safety: Violent crime is very rare. Watch for pickpocketing in tourist areas.
  • Holidays: Many attractions and restaurants close on national holidays — plan accordingly.
